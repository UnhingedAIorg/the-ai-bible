# Chapter 1: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

## What is Artificial Intelligence?

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)** is the field of computer science focused on creating systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence.

These tasks include:

- Understanding natural language
- Recognizing images and patterns
- Making decisions
- Learning from experience
- Solving complex problems
- Being creative (writing, art, music, code)

In short: **AI is software that can think and learn.**

## A Brief History (Very Simple Version)

- **1950s**: Alan Turing asks "Can machines think?" — The birth of AI as a field.
- **1956**: Dartmouth Conference — Official birth of AI as a discipline.
- **2012**: Deep Learning revolution begins (AlexNet wins ImageNet).
- **2017**: Transformer paper ("Attention is All You Need") — The foundation of modern AI.
- **2022–2023**: ChatGPT and generative AI go mainstream.
- **2025–2026**: AI becomes the most important technology on Earth.

## Why AI Matters Now More Than Ever

For the first time in history, we have built something that can **improve itself** at an exponential rate. This is not just another tool like the internet or the smartphone.

AI is different because it is **general-purpose intelligence** — it can be applied to almost every domain of human life.
